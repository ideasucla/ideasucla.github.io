# Executive Order Resources
These resources aim to explain the executive orders that Donald 
Trump has put out since taking office. The links dissect what orders are illegal and likely to be challenged, and how and where the orders are meant to affect immigrant communities.

- National Immigration Project: [Community Explainer](https://nipnlg.org/sites/default/files/2025-01/2025_NIPNLG-Trump-Week1.pdf?link_id=0&can_id=323990e77015cbec59713f71d42f4f1a&source=email-follow-up-resources-webinar-immigration-executive-orders-analysis-field-protection-strategies&email_referrer=email_2596793&email_subject=webinar-resources-immigration-executive-orders-analysis-field-protection-strategies) on Trump's week 1 actions.
- [Trump’s Day 1 Executive Orders](https://immigrantjusticenetwork.org/resources/trumps-day-1-executive-ordersfocus-on-criminalization-and-detention/?link_id=1&can_id=323990e77015cbec59713f71d42f4f1a&source=email-follow-up-resources-webinar-immigration-executive-orders-analysis-field-protection-strategies&email_referrer=email_2596793&email_subject=webinar-resources-immigration-executive-orders-analysis-field-protection-strategies): This document explains the EOs and outlines which parts use contact with the criminal system and immigration detention to further criminalize, detain and deport immigrants. 
- [NILC Analysis of Trump Day 1 Executive Orders: Unconstitutional, Illegal, and Cruel](https://www.nilc.org/articles/analysis-of-trump-day-1-executive-orders-unconstitutional-illegal-and-cruel/?link_id=2&can_id=323990e77015cbec59713f71d42f4f1a&source=email-follow-up-resources-webinar-immigration-executive-orders-analysis-field-protection-strategies&email_referrer=email_2596793&email_subject=webinar-resources-immigration-executive-orders-analysis-field-protection-strategies).
- [Immigration Policy Tracking Project](https://immpolicytracking.org/): A searchable compilation of Trump policies and executive actions from both of his terms.

# Opportunity for All Campaign
The Opportunity for All campaign (Assembly Bill 713) aims to correct the current interpretation of the law, allowing undocumented students to be hired in all California state schools, including the University of California campuses, California State Universities, and California Community Colleges. You learn more about the history of this campaign [here](https://www.weareijac.org/background-and-campaign-history).

# Immigrant Justic in Action Coalition
>The Immigrant Justice in Action Coalition (IJAC) is a statewide, immigrant-led organization in California advancing intersectional immigrant justice. Our work includes advocacy, organizing, legal strategies, leadership development, and community care rooted in equity, solidarity, and self-determination. IJAC operates as a grassroots, member-driven coalition guided by undocumented and mixed-status leadership.

IJAC is committed to uplifting undocumented communities, allies, and aligned organizations by amplifying voices, sharing undocumented narratives, and cultivating transformative relationships — all in pursuit of abolition, justice, and love.
