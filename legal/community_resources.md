# Community Resources
Non-profits, legal organizations, community coalitions and other repositories based around immigrant communities. These resources are geared towards uplifting, protecting, and strengthening communities.

- **Coalition for Humane Immigrant Rights Los Angeles [(CHIRLA)](https://www.chirla.org/)** provides a number of different resources (in person and online) for immigrants. This includes legalization workshops, KYR workshops, legal services, and relief services. They are also the legal service providers for numerous community college and CSU campuses.
- **Immigrant Legal Resource Center [(ILRC)](https://www.ilrc.org/)** Wide-ranging resource dealing with everything from asylum to removal defense. Includes books and trainings, community resources, and a resource directory to help folks find tools specific to their individual situations. 
- **ILRC Know Your Rights [Cards](https://www.ilrc.org/redcards)** Organizations can order cards or use the printable templates at the bottom of the pages. These cards are translated into 14 different languages.
- **[The National Immigration Law Center](https://www.nilc.org/)** Has a [repository](https://www.nilc.org/resources/) of general information that can help immigrants understand their rights. Embedded within the website are translations in Spanish of some of their fact sheets.
- **[Pathway for Immigrant Workers](https://www.myimmigrantpathway.org/legal-services)** Free immigration consultations for immigrant workers with DACA, TPS, or Asylum. 
- **[Immigrants Rising](https://immigrantsrising.org/)** Provides wealth of resources related to higher education, financial aid, and mental health and well-being.
- **[UndocuBlack Network](https://undocublack.org/)** A network fighting to create space for currently and formerly undocumented Black immigrants to not only survive but thrive. Their website provides videos, workshops, reports that help understand the intersection of Black Identity and the immigrant experience. 
- **Family Separation in the Sunshine State** Eleven [action steps](https://famsepfl.org/) to reduce the effects of family separation.
- **Crowdsource List** Immigrant support/advocacy and organizing groups [list](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/119BLag8Db_b3p6RRk5wfCTyMQB8YIxZkL-hzbRa2GzQ/edit?gid=0#gid=0), organized by state. 
- **Opening Doors, Inc** Northcal [org](https://openingdoorsinc.org/) for immigrants, refugees, and survivors of trafficking.
