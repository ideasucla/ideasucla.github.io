# University of California Resources
Information on students’ rights, campus policies/ protocols, and existing on-campus resources for students on the 
University of California campuses.

- How to respond to Immigrant Officers on campus on UCLA campus [infographic](https://ucla.app.box.com/s/knmzvrnvpo1xzxz75dl0rzz4coedbc2a)
    - Includes phone numbers to call in case of ICE action
    - UCLA Office of Campus Legal Counsel: (310) 206-6985
    - UC Immigrant Legal Services Attorney, Habiba Simjee: (424) 443-9653
    - Special Advisor to the Chancellor on Immigration Policy, Abel Valenzuela: (310) 206-5520 (can activate UCLA Detainment Response Protocol)
- AW 4811 Hotline for members facing an immigration emergency to legal resources : 888-416-2110
- Know Your Rights [ICE on campus](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1WnvKbEf0VsayB9HSyOnIKpk4h_aS2B7yG3gggrYcyuM/edit?tab=t.vp8ncosqznpq#heading=h.tu7qh2wzwynk): red cards, types of warrants, upcoming trainings, general resources. 
- [UC students Know Your Rights](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1-MBmgF1zTz1zehq6J5_qfXYa9WEZ2Vex/view)
- [UCLA Dream Resource Center](https://labor.ucla.edu/our-projects/dream-resource-center/): Program from the UCLA Labor Center focusing on social justice, equity, immigrant rights.
- [Undocumented Student Program](https://usp.ucla.edu/): UCLA program through the Bruin Resource Center that supports UCLA undergraduate and graduate students who are undocumented and/or part of mixed-status families.
- [UC Immigrant Legal Services Center](https://ucimm.sf.ucdavis.edu/): Free, high quality legal services for students at nine UC campuses.
- [UCLA EDI](https://equity.ucla.edu/)
- [UC Statement](https://www.universityofcalifornia.edu/sites/default/files/Statement-of-Principles-in-Support-of-Undocumented-Members-of-UC.pdf) of Principles in Support of Undocumented
Members of the UC Community.
- [UC FAQs](https://www.universityofcalifornia.edu/frequently-asked-questions-university-employees-about-possible-federal-immigration-enforcement) for University Employees About Possible Federal Immigration Enforcement Actions on University Property.
